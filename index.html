Cuping Therapy

Cupping therapy is an ancient healing method that may ease back pain, neck pain, headaches and other issues. It uses suction to pull on your skin and increase blood flow to the affected area. Cupping causes bruising and can lead to skin infection. There’s mixed evidence on the benefits of cupping, but the treatment risks are generally low.

Dry Needling Therapy

Dry needling is a treatment that healthcare providers use for pain and movement issues associated with myofascial trigger points. With this technique, a provider inserts thin needles into or near your trigger points. The needles stimulate your muscles, which causes them to contract or twitch. This helps relieve pain and improve your range of motion.

IASTM Therapy

Instrument-assisted soft tissue mobilization (IASTM) is a physical therapy technique that uses specialized tools to treat soft tissue and musculoskeletal issues. It's a popular manual therapy technique that can help with pain, mobility, and healing.


Chiropractic

Chiropractic therapy is a type of alternative medicine that uses the hands to treat musculoskeletal issues. It's often used to treat back pain, neck pain, and headaches.



Taping Therapy

Taping therapy is a technique that uses tape to treat muscles, joints, and tendons. It can help with pain, mobility, and stability.

Neck Pain

Neck pain can occur due to a number of reasons, including injuries, poor posture, or diseases. Most neck pain is not serious and clears up on its own in a few days.

Shoulder Pain

Shoulder pain can occur due to a number of possible reasons, including injuries, arthritis, or tendon issues. The pain can feel dull, sharp, achy, mild, or severe. It can also come on gradually or suddenly

Frozen Shoulder

Frozen shoulder is a condition that causes pain and stiffness in the shoulder joint. It's also known as adhesive capsulitis or shoulder contracture.

Tennis Elbow

Tennis elbow is a painful condition that causes inflammation of the tendons in the elbow. It's also known as lateral epicondylitis.

Golfers Elbow

Golfer's elbow, also known as medial epicondylitis, is a condition that causes pain and inflammation of the tendons on the inside of the elbow. It's often caused by repetitive motions of the wrist and forearm.

Carpal Tunnel syndrome

Carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS) occurs when the median nerve in your wrist is compressed. This can lead to pain, tingling, numbness, and weakness in your hand and fingers.

Scoliosis

Scoliosis is a sideways curve of the spine that can appear C- or S-shaped. It can affect people of any age, but most often starts in children between the ages of 10 and 15.

Kyphosis

Kyphosis is a spinal deformity that causes an abnormal forward curve in the upper spine. It can make the upper back appear rounded or hunched.

Low Back pain

Low back pain, also known as lumbago, is a common disorder that affects the muscles, nerves, and bones of the lower back. It can feel like a dull ache or a sharp pain.

Sciatica

Sciatica refers to pain that travels along the path of the sciatic nerve. The sciatic nerve travels from the buttocks and down each leg. Sciatica most often happens when a herniated disk or an overgrowth of bone puts pressure on the lumbar spine nerve roots. This happens "upstream" from the sciatic nerve.

Knee pain / TKR

Knee pain can occur due to a number of reasons, including injury, aging, or medical conditions. It can be a mild discomfort or debilitating

ACL/PCL/MCL/MENISCUS

ACL, MCL and PCL injuries are damage to ligaments that keep the knee from wobbling or giving out when you move. These ligaments are the anterior cruciate ligament (ACL), medial collateral ligament (MCL) and posterior cruciate ligament (PCL).

Disk herniation

A disc herniation, also known as a slipped or ruptured disc, occurs when a spinal disc bulges out and presses on nerves. It's a common back problem that can cause pain, numbness, or weakness in the arms or legs.

Plantar Fasciitis

Plantar fasciitis is inflammation of the plantar fascia, the tissue that connects your heel bone to your toes. It's a common cause of heel pain.

Ankle Sprain

An ankle sprain is a tear or stretch in the ligaments of the ankle. It's a common injury that occurs when the ankle rolls inward or twists suddenly.

Stroke

A stroke, also known as a brain attack or brain stroke, is a medical emergency that occurs when blood flow to the brain is disrupted. It can cause lasting brain damage, disability, or even death.

Paralysis

Paralysis is the loss of movement in part or all of the body. It can affect the face, arms, hands, legs, or trunk. Paralysis can be temporary or permanent.

Asthma/Copd

Asthma is a chronic lung disease that makes it hard to breathe. It's caused by inflammation of the airways, which can narrow and swell.
